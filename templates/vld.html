Voice Liveness Detection

How to Use ?

Voice liveness detection is a technology used to determine whether an audio input is coming from a live human source or from a recorded or synthetic source, such as a playback of a recording or a computer-generated voice. The primary goal of voice liveness detection is to enhance the security and accuracy of various applications that rely on voice authentication, speaker verification, or voice recognition systems. The importance of voice liveness detection arises from the potential vulnerabilities of voice-based authentication systems to spoofing attacks. Spoofing involves using pre-recorded audio or synthetic voices to trick a system into believing that the attacker's voice is genuine. Voice liveness detection aims to distinguish between real human voices and non-live sources to mitigate such attacks.
